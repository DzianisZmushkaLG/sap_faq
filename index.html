What is SAP S/4HANA Cloud? As of May 2021, SAP S/4HANA Cloud supports Multi-Cloud Scenarios, and the offer is based upon two different points of departure:
Public Cloud: typical targets are Customer owning the ability and mindset for transformational projects, with low landscape complexity and the potential to use a greenfield approach (Examples: digital businesses and service industries). SAP offers a complete, modern, SaaS ERP solution with all the benefits of the public cloud, an accelerated path to innovation and the lowest total cost of ownership, a clean cloud ERP solution that doesn’t require converting outdated and legacy ERP processes and configurations, Standardized best practices to reimagine business processes. Public Cloud is currently offered as Essentials edition (ES) and Extended edition (EX).
Private Cloud: typical targets are Customer owning the plans for an evolutionary journey to the cloud, High landscape complexity with mostly fragmented and highly customized systems (Example: large global manufacturers). SAP offers a rapid conversion of SAP ERP and SAP ECC to a modern, cloud-based architecture full enterprise management scope that includes a subscription and low total cost of ownership through cloud economics, perpetual license purchase option, SaaS landscape adoption at your own pace, Full, extensive, ERP functionality, including partner add-ons with the ability to extend and enhance. Do we need separate transports for country-specific customizing? For SAP Central Business Configuration, there is one transport request per milestone as shown in the demo. For configurations via self-service configuration user interfaces (SSCUIs), you are free to create separate transports. However, if there are multiple transports, you have to be aware of the dependencies. There will be a minimum of 2 transports – one with SAP Central Business Configuration and one with SSCUI.
SAP recommends that you start with one “default customizing” request and there can be only one “default customizing” request. For urgent changes, you can use additional transport requests. And in principle, you can also create a transport per country, LoB, etc. But we recommend separate transports only if you are sure that the configuration is independent from each other.
